Engineering

In terms of the selection of strains, we recorded the culture conditions and metabolic pathways of different strains through the BACDIVE database and Web of Science literature search, and screened out strains that met our requirements and had symbiotic potential. The screening requirements are as follows:

  • 1. Extracellular electrons can be used, or light energy can be used.
  • 2. The strain needs two kinds of energy, one that can fix carbon dioxide and one that can use nitrogen.
  • 3. The best oxygen tolerance is facultative anaerobic, and low oxygen concentration is in line with the growth characteristics of most of the current nitrogen-fixing microorganisms, and also conducive to our experiment to simulate this condition.

After actual investigation, the following strains of oxygen tolerance can be put into use:

  • Facultative anaerobic bacteria:2%-21%
  • Facultative aerobes: 1%-21% or even higher
  • Microanaerobic bacteria: 1%-10%
  • Microaerobes: 5%-21%
  • Microair tolerant bacteria: 2%-10%
  • Air-tolerant bacteria: Oxygen concentration has no effect and does not depend on oxygen to survive.

The physiological conditions and metabolic pathways of all the above strains were summarized, and strains with similar growth conditions and complementary metabolic pathways were selected in order to find the most symbiotic potential as our chassis cells.

After further investigation, we pre-selected Rolstonia, microalgae, cyanobacteria as carbon fixation module, Vickers as nitrogen fixation module, and then carried out a deeper investigation of these bacteria, and selected several subspecies with excellent properties:

Azotobacter vickeri is a kind of aerobic bacteria, which is different from other azotobacter, which broadens our selection range. It has three kinds of azotobacter enzymes, and has good nitrogen fixation and ammonia production capacity; It has a TCA cycle metabolic pathway, can utilize a wide range of carbon sources, and has a series of synthetic biology tools and mature gene editing methods, so we decided to officially choose azotobacter Vickerii as our chassis cells.

Rolstonia has good carbon sequestration ability and complete synthetic biology tools, but because it is a facultative anaerobes, it is unable to use its full productive capacity under aerobic conditions. Compared with microalgae, cyanobacteria has a higher photosynthetic efficiency and is genetically easy to handle. Besides, Synechococcus elongatus PCC 7942 can make use of exogenous sucrose transfer gene (CscB) to secrete sucrose to meet the growth requirements of Azotobacter wickerii. On this basis, we can optimize it to achieve better culture effect and production capacity. Therefore, cyanobacteria were finally selected as our chassis cells.
